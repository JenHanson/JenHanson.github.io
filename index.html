The Blender

Project Description:

The Blender is an award winning combat robot that has competed in various Battlebots and BotsIQ competitions from 2004 - 2015. Weighing in at 120 pounds, the Blender is a full body spinner, rotating a 30 inch bladed titanium shell at 1000 rpm for anyone brave enough to turn it on. Beginning life at the Richardson High School Robotics Magnet in 2004, the original Blender did well in it's first Battlebots IQ competition, taking home the coveted Coolest Robot award in it's first year. After this and every competition, the robot was taken back to the drawing board and improved. Our constant iterating let us take us take 1st place in both 2007 and 2009. In 2008 the Blender design went to college with the introduction of the Blender Mini and the UTDallas Battlebots Team. After winning the 120 lb high school division twice the the 120lb Blender design was brought to UTD to compete in even harder competitions. Today, the UTDallas Battlebots team Matt created continues to compete and the Blender most recently was awarded quarter finalist at RoboGames 2015 and 1st at BotsIQ 2011.

Project Details:

  • Competitions: Battlebots, BattlebotsIQ, BotsIQ, and RoboGames
  • Past Positions: Captain, Mechanical Design, Electrical Design, Driver
  • Date:2004 - Present

Zeno Prototypes

Project Description:

A walking and talking 17-inch-tall, 4.5-pound humanoid robot boy named Zeno. The prototype reperesented the first intelligent "conversational robot" and was intended to be the first of Hanson's "Robokind" line of personal, interactive bots. Zeno had 12 motors in the head that provided Zeno with a wide range of expressions and 18 motors used in the body for walking, talking, and interacting with the enviroment. Behind one of its eyes was a camera that the robot used to recognize faces and human expressions. The robot A.I. was driven by scripted content, fuzzy-logic, and voice recognition to keep users fed with relevant and engaging information.

Project Details:

  • Client: Hanson Robotics
  • Roles: Electrical Design, Mechanical Design
  • Date: 2007

Hydrophone Tester

Project Description:

The Infinity Seismic Hydrophone Tester ruggedly tests a vast and growing library of hydrophone sensors for geophysical exploration companies around the world. Hydrophones are underwater microphones sometimes used in reflection seismology to map sea-bed oil and mineral deposits. By testing hydrophones as they are deployed and recovered, proactive care can be taken to prevent and remove incorrect data from seismic shots. The quick-changeable hydrophone sealing adapters, a pressure washable exterier, and mud-resistant redundant test sensors are some of the reasons the Checkmate Marine is the industries foremost hydrophone tester.

Project Details:

  • Client: Vercét LLC and Infinity Seismic
  • Services: Mechanical Design, Research
  • Date: 2010

Spherical Ice Press

Project Description:

The Ice Press takes irregularly shaped blocks of ice and rapidly melts them into perfect 70mm ice spheres for use in fine spirits. When compared to other shapes, spherical geometry has the highest thermal mass when compared to surface area. This results in longer-lasting and slower melting ice keeping your drink cold and yet not watered down.

Project Details:

  • Client: HG Supply Company through Vercét LLC
  • Services: Mechanical Design and Manufacturing
  • Date: 2013
